Gradient-free Policy Architecture Search and Adaptation

Sayna Ebrahimi        Anna Rohrbach         Trevor Darrell

Conference on Robot Learning, 2017

Abstract

We develop a method for policy architecture search and adaptation via gradient-free optimization, learning optimal policy structure and parameters for autonomous driving tasks. Our model learns from both demonstration and environmental reward; gradient-free optimization allows optimizing architectures during behavioral cloning and then refining learned models using reward-based optimization. We learn an architecture of appropriate complexity to perceive aspects of world state relevant to the expert demonstration. Our model mitigates the effect of domain-shift during deployment by adapting a policy demonstrated in a source domain to the rewards obtained in a target environment. We show that our model learns more safely when leveraging human demonstration while optimizing for reward, and minimizes a cumulative crash metric over the agent's lifetime as it learns to drive.
